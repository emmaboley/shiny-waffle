How common is maternal perinatal depression and anxiety?

Minimal or mild symptoms of anxiety or depression that may sometimes be referred to as “baby blues” are extremely common occurring in approximately 85 to 90% of parents.

During the prenatal period, 10-20% of mothers experience depressive symptoms while a wider range of 9.5% to 29% experience anxiety symptoms.

Postpartum Depression affects 17.7% of women globally.

If you think that you may be suffering from any kind of Perinatal Mood Disorder, please reach out to your healthcare provider for a formal diagnosis or assistance. If unsure of where to turn to for help, Postpartum Support International's Non-Emergency Helpline (1-800-944-4773) can help you to establish a plan of action.

If you or someone you know is struggling with a perinatal mood disorder, help is out there. Postpartum Support International provides a wide variety of resources for those in need of help at their site.
